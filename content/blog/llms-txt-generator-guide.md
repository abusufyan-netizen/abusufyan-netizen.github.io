## Introduction

The [llms.txt Generator](/tools/llms-txt-generator/) is a specialized utility designed to help website owners implement the emerging `llms.txt` standard. This standard provides structured context to Large Language Models (LLMs) and AI crawlers, ensuring your site is accurately understood and cited in AI-generated answers.

This guide walks you through every feature of the generator and how to deploy the resulting file.

## Why Use the Generator?

While you can write an `llms.txt` file manually in a text editor, the generator ensures:
- **Valid Markdown Syntax:** Prevents formatting errors that could confuse parsers.
- **Protocol Compliance:** Follows the established structure (H1, Blockquote, Metadata, Sections).
- **Comprehensive Metadata:** Prompts you for critical info like AI contacts and indexing policies.
- **Speed:** Build a production-ready file in under 60 seconds.

## Step-by-Step Configuration

### 1. Basic Site Information
Start by entering your site's name and full URL. These form the primary identity of the file.

### 2. The AI-First Description
The "Description" field is the most important part of the file. It is typically rendered as a blockquote at the top of the file. 
- **Pro Tip:** Write this as if you are explaining your site to a highly intelligent assistant. Be literal and descriptive. Avoid marketing fluff.

### 3. AI Indexing Policy
Choose whether you want to explicitly allow or disallow AI models from indexing your content. 
- **Allowed:** Tells models like ChatGPT and Perplexity they have permission to cite and summarize your pages.
- **Disallowed:** Requests that models avoid using your content for training or search answers (Note: This is a policy, not a technical block like robots.txt).

### 4. Custom Sections
The generator allows you to add custom H2 sections. Use these to categorize your content (e.g., "Product Categories," "Recent Research," "Tutorial Guides").

## Exporting and Deployment

Once you are satisfied with the preview:
1. Click **Copy to Clipboard**.
2. Create a new file named `llms.txt` in your local text editor.
3. Paste the content and save.
4. Upload the file to the **root directory** of your website (e.g., `yoursite.com/llms.txt`).

## Verifying Your File

After uploading, visit the URL in your browser. You should see the plain text Markdown. You can also use AI tools like Perplexity or ChatGPT (with browsing) and ask them: "What does the llms.txt file on [yourdomain.com] say?" to verify they can read and interpret it.
